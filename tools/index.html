Free Tools

Browser-based exploratory tools for understanding sound

What These Are

Simple, focused tools to help you see and understand the physics underneath musical experiences. No sign-ups, no tracking, no subscriptions. Just open a page and explore.

Philosophy: These aren't meant to replace instruments or make you a "better musician." They're meant to demystify sound and make the invisible visible.

Available Tools

MIDI Visualizer

Connect your MIDI keyboard and see notes, frequencies, and MIDI numbers in real-time. Great for understanding the relationship between what you play and the underlying physics.

What you'll learn: Note names, fundamental frequencies, MIDI note numbers, velocity

Try it now →

Piano Chord Explorer (Coming Soon)

Visualize any chord on a piano keyboard with spectrum-based coloring for embodied learning. See chord structures through patterns, explore music theory with body-centered color awareness, and understand intervals through visual and kinesthetic anchors.

What you'll learn: Chord construction, intervals, embodied music theory, piano keyboard layout

Frequency Analyzer (Coming Soon)

Use your microphone to see the fundamental frequency and harmonics of any sound. Sing, hum, play an instrument - see what your browser hears.

What you'll learn: Fundamental frequency (f₀), harmonic series, formants

Interval Explorer (Coming Soon)

Play two notes and see the frequency ratio between them. Understand why a perfect fifth (3:2) feels stable while a tritone (45:32) creates tension.

What you'll learn: Frequency ratios, consonance vs dissonance, just intonation

Simple Synthesizer (Coming Soon)

Build sounds from scratch. Adjust oscillator types, frequencies, and envelopes to understand how synthesis works at a fundamental level.

What you'll learn: Oscillators, waveforms, ADSR envelopes, filters

Why Browser-Based?

No installation, no compatibility issues, no subscription fees. These tools run entirely in your browser using Web Audio API and Web MIDI API.

Privacy: Everything happens locally. Nothing is recorded, tracked, or sent to any server. Your audio and MIDI data never leave your device.

Open Source: All code is available on GitHub. Want to learn how they work? Fork them, modify them, learn from them.

Contributing

Have an idea for a tool? Built something useful? We'd love to include it.

Guidelines:

  • Keep it simple and focused on one concept
  • Make it visually calm (low-stimulation design)
  • Ensure it's fully accessible (keyboard nav, screen readers)
  • No tracking, no data collection, no external dependencies when possible
  • Include educational context (what you'll learn, why it matters)

Open an issue or PR on GitHub to get started.
