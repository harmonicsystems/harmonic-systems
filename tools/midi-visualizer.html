About This Tool

This is a simple MIDI visualizer that helps you see the relationship between what you play and the underlying physics of sound.

What you'll see:

  • The note name (e.g., "A4", "C#3")
  • The fundamental frequency in Hertz
  • The MIDI note number (0-127)
  • Velocity (how hard you hit the key)

Why this matters: Music is physics. Every note you play has a measurable frequency. Understanding this connection helps demystify music theory and shows how different tuning systems work.

Privacy: All processing happens in your browser. Nothing is recorded or sent anywhere. Your MIDI data never leaves your device.

Troubleshooting

No MIDI devices showing?

  • Make sure your MIDI keyboard is plugged in
  • Try refreshing the page
  • Check that your browser supports Web MIDI API (Chrome, Edge, Opera work well)
  • Safari requires macOS 12+ and may need permissions enabled
